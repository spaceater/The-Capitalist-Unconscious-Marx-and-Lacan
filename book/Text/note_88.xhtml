在评论一个阐述了“强加词汇”的症状的精神病案例时，拉康将语言比作癌症：“为什么没人觉得我们所依赖的词语在某种程度上是强加给我们的？”这样一来，那些通常所谓的病人有时会比健康的人走得更远。问题是，为什么所谓的正常人没有意识到语言是一种寄生虫，一种瘟疫，一种影响人类的癌症？”——雅克·拉康，《研讨会22：圣状》，巴黎：苏伊尔版，2005年，95。
Commenting on a case of psychosis, which articulated the symptom of ‘imposed words’, Lacan compared language with cancer: ‘How come that everyone does not feel that the words, on which we rely, are, in a way, imposed on us? In this way those usually called patients sometimes go much further than a healthy person. The question is, rather, why a so-called normal person does not perceive that speech is a parasite, a plague, a form of cancer that affects the human being?’ J. Lacan, Le Seminaire, livre XXII, Le sinthome, Paris: Editions du Seuil, 2005, 95.
